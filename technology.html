Six-Stage Condensation Process

Engineered for maximum efficiency and water quality

1

Steam Capture

Strategically positioned collection hoods capture steam from brewing vessels, fermentation tanks, and CIP systems. Variable-speed fans adjust automatically to maintain optimal capture rates while minimizing energy consumption.

Capture Efficiency: 85-95%
Fan Control: Variable Speed Drive
2

Primary Condensation

Steam enters our primary heat exchanger where controlled cooling begins the condensation process. Counter-flow design maximizes heat transfer efficiency while maintaining consistent water quality.

Heat Transfer: Counter-flow Design
Material: 316L Stainless Steel
3

Heat Recovery

Recovered thermal energy preheats incoming process water, reducing boiler load and overall energy consumption. This stage typically recovers 15-25% of the original thermal energy.

Recovery Rate: 70-85%
Energy Savings: 15-25%
4

Secondary Condensation

Final condensation occurs in our polishing heat exchanger, ensuring maximum water recovery while maintaining precise temperature control for optimal water quality.

Final Recovery: 80-90%
Temperature Control: ±2°C
5

Water Treatment

Condensed water passes through multi-stage filtration including activated carbon, UV sterilization, and optional reverse osmosis for applications requiring ultra-pure water.

Filtration: Multi-stage
UV Dose: 40 mJ/cm²
6

Storage & Distribution

Treated water is stored in food-grade tanks with automated quality monitoring. Distribution pumps deliver water on-demand to designated reuse applications.

Storage: Food-grade Tanks
Monitoring: Continuous
